SeaFood Paella

Description

We do not know when seafood paella was born or if it emerged at the same time as traditional Valencian paella; it is believed that it replaced meat ingredients with seafood. The richness of the Mediterranean Sea allowed fishermen to take advantage of the ingredients to make this exquisite alternative.

Today we will use Albufera rice, a recently appeared variety, created in Valencia and grown only in the Albufera Natural Park. The Albufera variety rice is a cross between Bomba rice and Senia rice, which brings together the best of each, that is, it resists excessive cooking and absorbs a great flavor. If we can't find this rice, don't worry, we can replace it with bomb rice.

Ingredients

  • 100 ml extra virgin olive oil
  • 4 langoustines
  • 4 prawns
  • 200 g chopped squid
  • 8 mussels
  • Sweet paprika
  • 200 g crushed tomato
  • 1.6 liters fish or seafood broth
  • Salt
  • 14 saffron threads
  • 400 g Albufera rice or Bomba rice
  • 150 g diced monkfish

Steps

  1. Level the paella and sear the langoustines and prawns.
  2. Incorporate the chopped squid and mussels and cook until they open.
  3. Cook the sofrito with sweet paprika and crushed tomato.
  4. Add the fish or seafood broth, adjust the salt, add the saffron, and when it boils, introduce the rice and cook it for 19 minutes.
  5. Reduce the heat after the first 6 minutes and add the diced monkfish.
  6. Incorporate the prawns, langoustines, and mussels. Reduce the heat to a minimum until the 19th minute.
